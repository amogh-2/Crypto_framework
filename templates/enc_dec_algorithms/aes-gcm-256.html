Use the encryption algorithm in GCM mode to securely encrypt and decrypt your files.

AES (Advanced Encryption Standard) in Galois/Counter Mode (GCM) is a secure and efficient encryption method that provides both confidentiality and authentication. With a 256-bit key, it offers strong protection against attacks while ensuring data integrity using an authentication tag. GCM operates in counter mode, making encryption fast and parallelizable, while Galois field multiplication ensures tamper detection. It is widely used in secure communications like TLS, VPNs, and encrypted storage. Proper nonce handling is essential for maintaining security.
